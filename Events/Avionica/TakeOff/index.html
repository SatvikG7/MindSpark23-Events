# Event

Take-off is an event that gives you an opportunity to showcase and implement your knowledge about the laws and concepts of physics that govern the motion of a rocket in the air, its take-off, its trajectory, etc.

# Sponsors

# Structure

Take-off consists of the participants using a pressure launcher to launch a water bottle rocket into the air. Participants have to construct their own water bottle rocket of a design of their choice. The event comprises three rounds which will be as follows-

Team

Maximum 3 per team.

Round 1:

The ground will be divided into six concentric zones from the launcher for this round. Further, the ground will be divided into 3 sectors, with each sector containing the mentioned 6 scoring zones. From the launching point, the participant will have to use the launcher to launch the water bottle rocket. The participants will have to choose one of the three sectors towards which they will be launching their rocket. The participant will be given points according to the area in which the rocket lands. If the rocket does not land in the sector chosen by the participant, no points will be awarded. The area and the points that the area holds will be visible during the event. This round will give 3 attempts to each team. The total score of the 3 attempts will be considered.

Round 2:

For this round, a target will be declared. Points will be awarded based on the distance between the rocket and the target. The point at which the rocket touches the ground for the first time will be considered, and subsequent bounces/sliding will not be considered.

Round 3:

In this round, the participants will have to attach a deployable parachute to the rocket using materials and design of their choice. Points will be awarded based on the time for which the rocket stays in the air after parachute deployment.

# Rules

  1. Participants will be required to bring their college ID cards.
  2. Participants will have to bring their own rockets made of plastic bottles and water. The launcher and pump will be provided by the organizing team of the event.
  3. Only the launcher provided by the organizing team must be used. Use of other launchers or hand-based launchers is not permitted.
  4. In Round 1, if the rocket falls on the boundary of two zones, then the points of the zone with higher points will be counted.
  5. Points will be counted only for the time of flight before the rocket touches the ground for the first time.
  6. In Round 3, participants will be disqualified in case the parachute doesn't open.
  7. The pump pressure can only be applied in the given range as specified by the organizing team.
